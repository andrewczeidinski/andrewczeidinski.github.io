##### Abstract:

Crystallization from solutions containing 2,2'-[naphthalene-1,8:4,5-bis(dicarboximide)-N,N'-diyl]-bis(ethylammonium) diiodide ((NDIC2)I2) and PbI2 has been investigated. Eight different materials are obtained, either by variation of crystallization conditions or by subsequent thermal or solvent-induced transformations. Crystal structures have been determined for five materials. Four of the structures form one-dimensional chains consisting of PbI6, and in one case PbI5(DMF), octahedra, either solely face-sharing or through a mixture of face-sharing and vertex-sharing. Another structure contains zero-dimensional clusters with three PbI6 octahedra and two unusually coordinated lead centers. Close contacts between iodide ions and the imide rings of NDIC2 suggest that iodide-to-NDIC2 charge-transfer interactions may be responsible for the red coloration of the materials. The optical and electrical properties of one material were studied, giving an absorption onset of 2.0 eV and a conductivity of 5.4 x 10^-5 +/- 1.1 x 10^-5 S m^-1.
